Cormorant fishing in Arashiyama

"Arashiyama cormorant fishing" is a traditional fishing method in which a cormorant fisherman, dressed in traditional attire consisting of a kazaore-eboshi hat and loincloth, pulls the reins from a boat and tames several cormorants to catch river fish such as sweetfish. It has a long history and has been practiced in Arashiyama for over 1,000 years. You can board a houseboat and see cormorant fishing up close. Shared boats are available only on the day. Reservations are required for exclusive use of the boat.

What is the Arashiyama Preservation Society?

The Arashiyama Preservation Society is an organization formed voluntarily by local residents to maintain the beauty of Arashiyama's natural scenery and tourist attractions, and to improve its appeal as a tourist destination. The society organizes events and cleanup activities that utilize the unique characteristics of the area. In the Arashiyama area, famous for its cherry blossoms in spring and autumn leaves in autumn, the Arashiyama Preservation Society takes the lead in holding various events throughout the year. Arashiyama Lantern Floating Festival

The Arashiyama Lantern Floating Festival originated in 1949 as a memorial service using lanterns to console the spirits of those who died in war. The spirits are sent to the Pure Land on lanterns.

Event period: August 16 time: 7pm to 9pm place: Arashiyama Nakanoshima Park Fee: free

Adashino Nenbutsu Temple Sento Kuyo

This is a religious event held every August where candles are lit to honor the several thousand abandoned Buddha statues enshrined on the riverbank in Saiin on the grounds of Nenbutsuji Temple.

Event period: Last Saturday and Sunday of August (2 days) time: 18:00-21:00 (reception hours 17:30-20:30) place: Adashino Nenbutsu Temple Fee: Adults: 1,000 yen (free for elementary school students and younger, but only if accompanied by a guardian)

Daikakuji Temple Moon Viewing Evening

The "Moon Viewing Evening" began when Emperor Saga floated a boat on Osawa Pond under the harvest moon and enjoyed playing with cultural figures and aristocrats. Look up at the sky and you'll see the moon shining brightly, and look down and you'll see the moon reflected on the calm water. En joy an elegant time watching these two moons at Osawa Pond, one of Japan's three great places to view the harvest moon.

Event period: Three days including Mid-Autumn Festival time: 17:30-21:00 (last entry at 20:30) place: Former Saga Imperial Palace Daihonzan Daikakuji Temple Osawa Pond Fee: 500 yen (even those who visit during the day will need to pay a separate admission fee)
